Privacy Policy

Last updated: 21 June 2026
Short version: Ramble is built so your journal stays on your device. We have no servers and no account system. We don't collect, sell, or track your data. The sections below explain the details, including the few optional features that involve a third party — and only when you turn them on.

1. Who we are

Ramble (“the app”, “we”) is a personal voice-journaling app for iOS and Android, developed by an independent developer. You can reach us at syroxivoss9@gmail.com.

2. Our core principle: no servers, no accounts

Ramble does not require you to create an account, sign in, or give us any personal information. By default, the app runs entirely on your device. Your recordings, transcripts, moods, categories, and notes are stored locally and are not sent to us — because we do not operate servers that receive them.

3. What stays on your device

  • Voice recordings — saved on your device and encrypted at rest using AES-256. The encryption key is stored in your device’s secure keystore (Apple Keychain / Android Keystore), not inside the file and not on any server.
  • Transcripts — generated on your device using the operating system’s on-device speech recognition. Transcript text is stored in an encrypted local database.
  • Moods, categories, titles, favorites, insights, settings — stored locally on your device.

4. Microphone and speech recognition

The app uses your microphone only while you are recording an entry, and only after you grant permission. Speech-to-text transcription is performed on your device by the operating system; on supported devices this happens without sending audio to a remote server. You can revoke microphone access at any time in your system settings.

5. Optional features that involve a third party

These features are off by default. They only do anything if you explicitly turn them on, and the app shows you what happens before you do.

  • “Song of the moment” search. If you search for a song to attach to an entry, your search text is sent to Deezer’s public search API to return matching tracks. No account or personal identifier is sent. If you don’t use song search, no such request is made.
  • Backups you create. You can export an encrypted, password-protected backup of your journal. The backup is created on your device and saved wherever you choose (for example Files, iCloud Drive, or Google Drive — your own storage). We never receive it. If you lose the password, the backup cannot be recovered by anyone, including us.
  • Optional cloud AI (if offered). If a cloud-powered summary feature is made available and you opt in, the specific transcripts you choose may be sent — through a request you initiate — to an AI provider to generate a summary, and you will be told this on a consent screen before it is enabled. It remains off unless you turn it on, and can be turned off at any time.

6. Purchases

Optional “Ramble Plus” purchases are processed by Apple (App Store) or Google (Google Play). We do not see or store your payment details. Apple and Google handle the transaction under their own privacy policies; the app only learns whether an entitlement is active.

7. What we do NOT do

  • No advertising and no ad identifiers.
  • No third-party analytics or behavioral tracking SDKs.
  • No selling or sharing of personal data.
  • No profiles built about you.

8. Data retention and deletion

Your data stays on your device until you delete it. You can delete individual entries in the app, or remove all data by deleting the app. Because we don’t hold your data on servers, there is nothing for us to delete on your behalf.

9. Children

Ramble is not directed at children under the age required by your local app store and applicable law. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

10. Your rights

Because your data lives on your device under your control, you can access, export, and delete it yourself at any time. If you have questions about your privacy or this policy, contact us at syroxivoss9@gmail.com.

11. Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we will revise the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, note the change in the app. Continued use after an update means you accept the revised policy.
